Live Trial Dashboard

Trial Research & Reporting Platform

Trial Consulting Firm — Civil Litigation

The Problem

The firm was running mock trial research using spreadsheets, manual tally sheets, and disconnected documents. Juror position data lived in one place. Survey responses in another. Verdict tracking happened on paper. Every deliverable was rebuilt from scratch for each case — costing hours that could have gone to analysis.

What Was Built

A full-stack web application with 32+ pages covering the entire mock trial research workflow. The platform tracks juror positions in real time, captures pre- and post-stimulus survey responses, generates interim verdict tables, and produces seating charts — all from a single data source.

Print-ready deliverables — damages charts, frequency analysis reports, deliberation summaries, and individual juror profile reports — are generated directly from the data. No copy-pasting. No reformatting. The consultant opens the page and the report is ready.

How It Works

Supabase handles the backend — juror records, survey responses, case metadata. The frontend is vanilla HTML and JavaScript deployed on Netlify. Chart.js powers the visualizations. PDF outputs use PyMuPDF for precise page-level control including headers, footers, and page numbering.

The system was built to be extended per case. New survey instruments, new chart types, new deliverable formats — each builds on the existing architecture without starting over.

The Result

Used during live mock trial proceedings. Deliverables that previously took hours are generated in seconds. The consultant focuses on analysis and client communication. The system handles the rest.

Automation & Integration

Practice-Wide Command Center

Trial Consulting Firm — Operations

The Problem

The firm's operational data was scattered across Clio, email, spreadsheets, and memory. Matter status lived in one person's head. Financial tracking was manual. Contracts went through DocuSign but the signed versions weren't linked back to the matter. Nothing talked to anything else.

What Was Built

A Notion-based command center that serves as the single source of truth for the entire practice. Every active matter, its financials, its contract status, its deadlines, and its team assignments — all in one view. Eight custom Zapier workflows connect Clio, DocuSign, and the firm's financial tracking into automated pipelines.

When a new matter is created in Clio, it appears in Notion. When a contract is signed in DocuSign, the matter record updates. When a payment is received, the financial status reflects it. No manual entry. No chasing.

How It Works

Clio serves as the matter management system of record. Zapier listens for triggers — new matters, status changes, payment events — and pushes updates to Notion databases with mapped field relationships. DocuSign completion webhooks update contract status. The Notion workspace is organized around the consultant's actual decision-making needs, not the software's default structure.

The Result

86 existing matters migrated on day one. The firm went from checking three systems to checking one. Matter intake, pipeline tracking, and financial reporting now run on the same infrastructure. The consultant stopped being an admin and went back to being a consultant.

Courtroom Technology

Live Jury Selection Dashboard

Trial Consulting Firm — Jury Selection

The Problem

Jury selection moves fast. The consulting team needed to rate jurors, track strikes, communicate internally, and reference demographic profiles — all while court was in session. Pen-and-paper couldn't keep up. Existing tools weren't built for the speed or the stakes.

What Was Built

A real-time jury selection dashboard deployed on Netlify and backed by Airtable. The team rates jurors on a shared interface. Strike tracking updates live. A built-in team chat keeps communication off-text and in context. Demographic profiles pull from pre-loaded juror questionnaire data.

The interface was designed for courtroom conditions — high contrast, large touch targets, fast load times, no unnecessary clicks. Every interaction was built around what the team needs to see and do in the next 30 seconds, not the next 30 minutes.

How It Works

Airtable serves as the live data layer — juror records, ratings, strike status, and chat messages all sync in real time. The frontend polls for updates on a tight interval. The dashboard was built, tested, and debugged during the actual proceeding — adjustments were made between panels based on what the team needed in the moment.

The Result

Used during an active federal court voir dire proceeding. The team operated from a single shared view instead of passing notes. Decisions were faster. Nothing was missed.

Billing & Workflow

Billing & Financial Workflow Rebuild

Trial Consulting Firm — Finance

The Problem

The firm's billing ran on a legacy time-tracking platform that had served its purpose but couldn't keep up with how the practice actually operated. Invoices required manual formatting. Subcontractor time entries came in late or inconsistently. The chart of accounts in QuickBooks didn't match the billing categories. Every invoice cycle was a project in itself.

What Was Built

A complete billing workflow redesign. The existing platform was audited to separate what was a process gap from what was a tool limitation. The invoicing flow was rebuilt on a modern platform with rate structures that match how the firm bills — by consultant, by phase, by matter type.

QuickBooks integration was configured so that invoice data flows directly into the books without manual re-entry. The chart of accounts was simplified to reflect actual business categories, not software defaults. Subcontractor time entry was standardized with clear cutoffs and compliance rules.

How It Works

Time entries flow from the billing platform to invoices. Invoices sync to QuickBooks. The principal reviews and approves — she doesn't proofread or reformat. Subcontractors follow a documented entry standard with weekly cutoffs. Late entries don't hold up the cycle.

The Result

Invoicing went from a multi-day process to a same-day workflow. The principal's role shifted from proofreader to approver. Financial reporting became consistent enough to actually use for decision-making.
